About devendra kula vellalar sangam delhi

14 years of unwavering commitment to those who need it most

Our Mission

To uplift the lives of underprivileged communities, persons with disabilities, and families of martyrs by providing holistic support in education, nutrition, healthcare, and economic empowerment — with dignity, compassion, and transparency.

Our Vision

A just and inclusive India where every individual — regardless of poverty, disability, or sacrifice — can live with dignity, access equal opportunities, and contribute meaningfully to society.

Our Values

  • Compassion & Empathy
  • Transparency & Accountability
  • Inclusivity & Non-discrimination
  • Community-led Action
  • Respect for Human Dignity

Our Story

Born From a Single Act of Kindness

devendra kula vellalar sangam delhi was founded in 2010 by Dr. Arjun Verma, a retired army doctor who witnessed firsthand the suffering of soldiers' families and rural communities with no access to basic services.

What began as a small community kitchen in Shamli has grown into a multi-program organisation spanning 120+ villages, 6 districts of Uttar Pradesh, helping over 50,000 beneficiaries each year.

We operate with minimal administrative overhead — over 85% of every donation goes directly to program activities. Our ground teams are local, our volunteers are passionate, and our resolve is unshakeable.

Our Milestones

2010

Foundation Established

Started with a community kitchen serving 50 meals daily in Shamli.

2012

Education Initiative Launched

Opened our first free learning centre serving 200 underprivileged children.

2015

PWD Program Started

Provided emergency food, oxygen, and medicines to 15,000+ families during pandemic.
